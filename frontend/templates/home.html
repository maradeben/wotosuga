According to the World Health Organization (WHO), diabetes is a major public health problem. In 2021, an estimated 463 million adults (aged 18 years and older) had diabetes. This number is expected to rise to 642 million by 2040.

There are a number of things that can be done to reduce the burden of diabetes. These include:

  • Early diagnosis and treatment: Early diagnosis and treatment of diabetes can help to prevent complications.
  • Prevention: There are a number of things that can be done to prevent diabetes, such as maintaining a healthy weight, being physically active, and eating a healthy diet.
  • Improving access to care: People with diabetes need access to quality care, including medication, regular checkups, and education.
  • Research: There is ongoing research into new treatments and prevention strategies for diabetes.
